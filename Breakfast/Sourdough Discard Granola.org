* Sourdough Discard Granola
** Ingredients:
- 60 g liquid fat (avocado oil, coconut oil, olive oil)
- 60 g liquid sweetener (maple syrup, honey, agave)
- 40 g light brown sugar (or coconut sugar)
- 100 g sourdough starter (active or discard)
- 10 g vanilla extract
- 200 g oats (old fashioned are best, but quick also works)
- 50 g seeds (pepitas, sunflower seeds, hemp seeds, flax seeds)
- 100 g nuts (pecans, almonds, walnuts, hazelnuts, pistachios)
- 100 g dried fruit (raisins, blueberries, cranberries, apricots, dates, cherries, pineapple; chop up larger dried fruit)
- 50 g shredded coconut (optional)
- 5 g sea salt
- 1 tsp cinnamon (or other preferred spices)
** Method:
1. Preheat oven to 325Â°F.
2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together 60 g liquid fat, 60 g liquid sweetener, 40 g light brown sugar, 100 g sourdough starter, and 10 g vanilla extract.
3. Then, mix in 200 g oats, 100 g seeds, 100 g nuts, 100 g dried fruit, 50 g shredded coconut, 5 g sea salt, and 5 g cinnamon (or other preferred spices).
4. Spray a half sheet pan with oil or line it with parchment paper.
5. Spread granola mixture in an even layer on sheet pan, pressing down with a spatula or the back of a measuring cup.
6. Compacting the mixture will encourage clumps.
7. Bake for 30 minutes, rotating sheet pan halfway through.
   #+begin_tip
   If you prefer clusters, do not break apart the mixture during baking. If you prefer a loose granola mixture, toss the mixture halfway through baking.
   #+end_tip
9. Let cool for 10 minutes before breaking apart into clumps.
10. Store in an airtight container (I prefer glass) at room temperature.
11. Keeps for up to 4 weeks.
